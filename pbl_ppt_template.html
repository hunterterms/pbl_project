Intelligent Campus
Marketplace

"UniHub bridges the digital gap in higher education by creating a trust-first, serverless ecosystem that centralizes peer-to-peer commerce and social engagement, exclusively for verified university students."

Problem Statement

University campuses suffer from digital fragmentation. Students must navigate a chaotic mix of physical boards, unstructured WhatsApp groups, and untrusted global platforms like Facebook Marketplace to trade items or find events.

This leads to high scam risks (stranger danger), inefficient resource circulation (textbooks going to waste), and a lack of specific academic context in search results.

Literature Review

Analyzed Facebook Marketplace & eBay: Found they lack student verification and hyper-local relevance. Analyzed Campus Portals (Blackboard): Found high security but poor UX for non-academic tasks.

Innovation / Research Gap

  • Identity-Layered Commerce: Uses Amazon Cognito to enforce ".edu" only access (Trust-First).
  • Context-Aware Search: DynamoDB schema optimized for Course Codes (e.g., "CS101") vs generic keywords.
  • Serverless Scale: Zero-cost idle time using AWS Lambda.
